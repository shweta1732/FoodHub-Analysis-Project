# FoodHub Data Analysis Project

## Project Context
The number of restaurants in New York is increasing day by day. Many students and busy professionals rely on online food delivery services due to their hectic lifestyles. FoodHub, a food aggregator company, offers access to multiple restaurants through a single smartphone app. The company wants to analyze customer order data to understand demand and enhance customer experience.

## Objective
As a Data Scientist, I performed data analysis to answer key questions about the FoodHub order data, aiming to help the company improve its business.

## Data Description
The dataset contains information related to food orders, including:
*   `order_id`: Unique ID of the order
*   `customer_id`: ID of the customer who ordered the food
*   `restaurant_name`: Name of the restaurant
*   `cuisine_type`: Cuisine ordered by the customer
*   `cost_of_the_order`: Cost of the order
*   `day_of_the_week`: Indicates whether the order is placed on a weekday or weekend
*   `rating`: Rating given by the customer out of 5
*   `food_preparation_time`: Time (in minutes) taken by the restaurant to prepare the food
*   `delivery_time`: Time (in minutes) taken by the delivery person to deliver the food

## Key Questions Addressed
1.  Number of rows and columns in the data.
2.  Datatypes of the different columns.
3.  Missing values in the data.
4.  Statistical summary of the data, especially food preparation time.
5.  Number of unrated orders.
6.  Distribution of all variables (univariate analysis).
7.  Top 5 restaurants by number of orders.
8.  Most popular cuisine on weekends.
9.  Percentage of orders costing more than $20.
10. Mean order delivery time.
11. IDs of the top 3 most frequent customers.
12. Relationships between important variables (multivariate analysis).
13. Restaurants eligible for promotional offers (rating count > 50 and average rating > 4).
14. Net revenue generated by the company.
15. Percentage of orders taking more than 60 minutes to deliver.
16. Variation in mean delivery time between weekdays and weekends.

## Key Findings & Conclusions
*   The dataset contains 1898 rows and 9 columns, with no missing or duplicate values initially. The 'rating' column was later transformed to numerical, converting 'Not given' to NaN.
*   Approximately 39% of orders lack customer ratings.
*   The average food preparation time is 27.37 minutes (ranging from 20 to 35 minutes), and the average delivery time is 24.16 minutes (ranging from 15 to 33 minutes).
*   American, Japanese, Italian, and Chinese cuisines are the most popular.
*   Weekend orders significantly outnumber weekday orders.
*   Shake Shack is the restaurant with the highest number of orders (219).
*   American cuisine is the most popular on weekends.
*   About 29.24% of orders cost more than $20.
*   The total average time for an order from placement to delivery is about 51.5 minutes.
*   Top 3 frequent customers have placed 13, 10, and 9 orders respectively.
*   No strong correlations were found between numerical variables.
*   Delivery times tend to be higher during weekdays compared to weekends.
*   Restaurants eligible for promotional offers (rating count > 50 and average rating > 4) include Blue Ribbon Fried Chicken, Blue Ribbon Sushi, Shake Shack, and The Meatball Shop.
*   The company's estimated net revenue is $5340.44.
*   Around 10.54% of orders take more than 60 minutes to deliver, with more such occurrences on weekdays.

## Recommendations
*   **Improve Rating Collection:** Implement strategies to encourage customers to rate their orders, given that ~39% are unrated. This could involve in-app nudges or incentives.
*   **Operational Efficiency for Popular Cuisines/Restaurants:** Since popular cuisines (American, Japanese, Italian) and top restaurants (Shake Shack, The Meatball Shop) show higher preparation and delivery times, consider recommendations to these partners regarding staffing, kitchen efficiency, or delivery logistics to manage high demand.
*   **Weekend vs. Weekday Optimization:** Capitalize on higher weekend demand. For weekdays, investigate the reasons for longer delivery times and higher proportion of orders exceeding 60 minutes. This might involve optimizing delivery routes or increasing driver availability during peak weekday hours.
*   **Promotional Offers:** Utilize the identified top-performing restaurants (e.g., Shake Shack, The Meatball Shop) in marketing campaigns and promotional offers to attract more customers.
*   **Customer Loyalty Program:** Continue with initiatives like offering discounts to frequent customers to reinforce loyalty.
